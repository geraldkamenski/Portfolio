Submersible
ROV

Remotely Operated Underwater Vehicle - Senior Design Project

Background

This project originated from the need for an affordable and adaptable underwater remotely operated vehicle suitable for academic research and experimentation. Commercial ROV systems are often cost prohibitive and limit customization, making them impractical for repeated use by student teams. The intent was to develop a proof of concept platform using off the shelf components and readily manufacturable parts that could serve as a reusable baseline for future cohorts. Emphasis was placed on modularity, reliability, and ease of modification so that subsequent teams could expand functionality without redesigning the core system.

Goal

The goal of this project was to design and build a versatile underwater ROV capable of stable maneuvering, near surface operation, and future subsystem expansion. The platform was intended to support applications such as underwater observation, autonomous pin point manuverability, and environmental interaction using mechanical arms.
