About Me

I am a Mechanical Engineering graduate with a strong interest in thermal systems, fluid mechanics, and hardware design. My experience spans CAD modeling, simulation, fabrication, and testing through academic projects, independent builds, and industry experience, including powertrain engineering work in a research and development environment. I enjoy solving practical engineering problems that require balancing performance, manufacturability, and reliability, and I am particularly motivated by projects that involve hands on design, iterative testing, and data driven decision making.


In my free time, I enjoy working on my car, focusing on maintenance and custom fabrication to improve quality of life on my 1986 Mercedes. I also play video games as a social and casual way to unwind and stay connected with friends. Outside of that, I enjoy staying active through recreational volleyball and snowboarding at the park in Big Bear, which gives me a good balance between problem solving and time outdoors.

Overview

October 2025 - December 2025

Graphics Card
External Cooling System

This project focuses on the design and simulation of a compact GPU cooling system that uses forced airflow and a liquid cold plate to reduce thermal buildup in high power graphics hardware. Using CAD modeling and flow simulation, the system evaluates temperature distribution, airflow behavior, and cooling effectiveness to demonstrate improved thermal performance compared to conventional air cooling alone.

ROV

Designed and integrated a small scale submersible ROV featuring a sealed pressure housing, multi thruster propulsion system, and Pixhawk based ArduSub control architecture with custom power distribution and wiring. Efficiency Testing
